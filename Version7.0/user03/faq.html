Are Clowns really venomous?
Since Clowns rarely bite, only doing so as a last resort, it's not too often that one would need to worry about a Clown's venom. Despite this, each species of Clown is in fact venomous to different degrees.

The venom of Whiteface and Auguste Clowns has a mild sting at worst.

Tramp and Rodeo Clown venom can cause a moderate pain for a minute or two.

Mime venom doesn't hurt too much, but it dramatically slow the victim's movements and reaction time.

The venom from a Creepy Clown causes a rush of adrenaline and increased fear, as well as sharp pain lasting up to 30 minutes.

A Jester's venom can be considered dangerous, as it contains an incredibly painful neurotoxin that can kill a person in large doses.

And lastly the Harlequin's venom, which is the most potent of clown species. Call emergency services immedaitely if bitten by a Harlequin, as brain death will occur within an hour of exposure.
